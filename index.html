Welcome to my (minimal) scientific homepage - Toni Ingolf Gossmann

I am a researcher in the field of evolutionary biology. I work on different topics that are all connected by my passion for knowledge and creativity. At the moment I am at Bielefeld University as an Independent Career Fellow.

Research Interests

My work focuses on approaches and data analysis to identify imprints of selection in natural populations.

Population Genomics
  • Great tits and Alpine marmots
  • Molecular mechanisms of fine scale recombination rate variation (Great tits)
  • Evolution of Methylation (Great tits)

Comparative Genomics

  • Complex trait evolution over macro-evolutionary time scales (Birds)
  • The evolution of protein structure
  • NAD metabolism

Functional Genomics

  • Time-trajectories to infer the strength of selection
  • The evolution of senescence
  • Reproductive proteomics (Zebra finch, Drosophila pseudoobscura)
